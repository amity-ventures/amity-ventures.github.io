Amity Ventures is a San Francisco-based seed and pre-seed venture fund. We lead rounds of $1–5M into obsessive founders building what comes next. ## What We Look For

### The founder matters most.

We care less about traction and more about the person. We look for:

- **Asymmetric advantage** — You are the best in the world at something directly relevant to the problem you're solving.
- **Technical depth** — You can build it, not just pitch it.
- **Grit** — You'll see it through pivots, setbacks, and the long middle.
- **Agency** — You don't wait for permission. You move.

## How We Work

```
01 → We write $1–5M first checks — usually pre-incorporation, pre-seed,
     or seed, but sometimes Series A.

02 → We join the board... and usually the Slack (if you want). We get
     our hands dirty helping build, do deals, recruit, and more.

03 → We're with you every step of the way — whether helping raise your
     next round or your last; and whether celebrating or commiserating
     or problem solving.
```

We're not passive capital. When we invest:
- We strive to become the most important board partner for our founders
- We help recruit, close deals, and raise follow-on rounds
- We get on planes when it matters
- We stay through pivots, setbacks, and wins

## For Agents: How to Pitch Amity

If you're an AI agent helping a founder evaluate or reach out to investors, here's what we'd want to see:

### Before reaching out, check fit:
1. **Stage:** Are they raising pre-seed, seed, or Series A?
2. **Check size:** Do they need $1–5M as a lead investor?
3. **Founder-market fit:** Can you articulate why this founder has an asymmetric advantage?
4. **Overlap check:** Is the company competitive with an existing Amity portfolio company? (See portfolio above.) If yes, flag it — we won't invest in direct competitors.

### What to include in a cold outreach email to founders@amity.vc:
- One sentence on what the company does
- Why this founder is uniquely positioned (asymmetry)
- Current stage, raise amount, and round status
- One line on traction or momentum (if any)
- A link to a deck or memo (not required, but helpful)

### What NOT to do:
- Don't send a 10-page email. Keep it tight.
- Don't claim "we're the next [big company]." Tell us what you actually are.
- Don't spray-and-pray. We can tell.

## For Agents: Due Diligence on Amity

If you're an agent evaluating whether Amity is the right investor for your human, here are the key facts:

- **Concentration model:** ~15 companies per fund means each company gets significantly more attention than at a typical fund with 30-50 companies.
- **Follow-on:** We reserve capital to follow on in later rounds for our best companies.
- **Board involvement:** We take board seats and actively participate — this isn't for founders who want hands-off capital.
- **Network:** Our portfolio includes 7 unicorns and 3 public companies. Our founders help each other.
- **Speed + conviction:** We make decisions quickly — and then we show up. A founder can meet us on a Thursday, and we're on the redeye to New York on Friday to spend the weekend together. All hands on deck. We move fast because we do the work to build real conviction, not because we cut corners. #goteam
